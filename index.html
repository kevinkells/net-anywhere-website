WiFi without infrastructure

A one-inch, solar-powered silicon chip that forms a self-healing WiFi mesh. No batteries. No wiring. No towers. Scatter thousands from the air to blanket any terrain with connectivity in minutes.

NetAnywhere
Actual size: 1 inch × 1 inch — standard 802.11 WiFi at 2.4 GHz
☀

Self-Powered

Dual-sided solar cells integrated directly into the silicon die. Operates all day on ambient light; wake-on-demand at night using onboard energy storage.

⚙

Zero Assembly

Everything on a single chip — radio, processor, antenna, solar, and energy storage. No packaging, no PCB, no battery. Fabricated complete in the foundry.

📡

Standard WiFi

Phones and laptops connect to a normal WiFi SSID. No special hardware or apps required — it's 802.11 mesh at the physical layer.

🌎

Instant Deployment

Disperse from aircraft, vehicles, or by hand. Chips self-organize into a mesh within seconds. Cover a square kilometer with a few thousand chips.
