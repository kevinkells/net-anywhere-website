Does the solar harvest → store → relay loop work at all? What range and power numbers do we actually see?

Demonstrated on hardware
  • BLE + WiFi simultaneous operation impossible on ESP32-C6 — WPA2 handshake fails under active BLE mesh traffic; time-multiplex workaround confirmed functional measuredMar 30, 2026
  • 4-node BLE mesh relay proven indoors: shielded node reached over 18 ft + 90° turn via relay measuredMar 31, 2026
  • Outdoor range at 56th & 1st Ave, NYC: 35 m at +3 dBm, 88 m at +20 dBm (1M PHY) measuredMar 31, 2026
Estimated from datasheets (not yet bench-measured)
  • BLE duty-cycled idle: ~0.42 mW target; continuous ESP-BLE-MESH: ~20 mW (ESP32-C6 datasheet)
  • WiFi soft-AP draw: ~200–400 mW burst; 10 F supercap sustains ~60–90 s window (calculated)
In progress
  • Solar wiring integration — harvest loop not yet closed on bench started Apr 1, 2026
  • Power-under-sun measurements pending (supercap charge rate, overnight survival)

"We have a working 4-node mesh with measured range-per-hop and power draw from real hardware in the field. The harvest loop is the next gate."

Does the schematic close on a properly designed PCB, with a solar cell sized to near-target dimensions?

Approach
  • ~40–50 mm square, standard 2-layer FR4, QFN / SOIC packages — no exotic assembly
  • Solar cell sized to approximate the 25 × 25 mm target harvest area
  • Fully probeable — validates harvester topology, supercap sizing, and firmware before committing to HDI
  • Fast-turn fabrication at PCBWay or equivalent: ~$10/board, ~1 week

"The breadboard proves the physics; the 2-layer PCB proves the schematic. We de-risk the circuit before committing to expensive HDI design rules."

I
Phase I — HDI Micro-PCBA
The "Micro-PCBA Sandwich"
A rigid square roughly the size of a Triscuit (~1.5 × 1.5 inches), about as thick as a stack of 3–4 credit cards. Solar film bonded to both faces. You can hold one between two fingers. This is the first unit built at close to the target footprint.
Planned
Estimated cost ~$2–5k
Duration 6–10 weeks
Est. completion Jul–Aug 2026

Does the power budget close when the node fits in the palm of your hand?

Architecture
  • 25 × 25 mm HDI PCB; WLCSP silicon (ESP32-C6 WLCSP or nRF52840 WLCSP)
  • PMIC: BQ25570 DSBGA or e-peas AEM10941; supercap: SMD EDLC, 0.9 mm profile
  • Thin-film PV bonded to top and bottom faces; total thickness ~3.5–4.2 mm
  • Assembly: standard SMT pick-and-place at MacroFab or Screaming Circuits

"We have a functional node at close to the target footprint that you can hold in your hand. The power budget closes. Shrinking from 4 mm to 1 mm is a manufacturing transition, not a physics question — and that is what Phase II funds."

II
Phase II — Bare Die on Flex
Chip-on-Flex (CoF)
Shrinks from the Triscuit to a Wheat Thin (1 inch × 1 inch) — and from a stack of credit cards down to one. Flexible substrate instead of rigid PCB. Solar film bonded to both faces. The unit bends slightly. At this thickness and weight, it can tumble and drift on the wind when dropped from altitude — the first unit truly suited to scatter deployment.
Planned
Estimated NRE $30–50k
Duration 3–6 months
Est. completion Q4 2026 – Q1 2027

Can we hit the ~1 mm Z-axis profile required for aerodynamic scatter deployment?

Architecture
  • 50 µm polyimide flex substrate with RDL antenna; bare dies via Micross / SemiDice
  • Energy storage: CAP-XX ultra-thin prismatic supercap pouch (~0.4 mm)
  • Passives: Silicon Capacitors (SiCaps) or 01005 for zero Z-height impact
  • Assembly at OSAT: QP Technologies or Promex Industries
  • Total target thickness: ~0.95–1.15 mm

"Phase I proved the physics. Phase II proves the form factor. The supply chain here maps directly to the high-volume reel-to-reel manufacturing that hits $1/node."

III
Phase III — Custom Silicon
Fan-Out Wafer-Level Packaging (FOWLP)
Visually identical to Phase II — a Wheat Thin, credit card thin — but now a single hermetically sealed package with no visible components, seams, or connectors. Both faces are photovoltaic. The only external surface is SiO₂ passivation. It looks and feels like a smooth, matte ceramic tile. Drop thousands from an aircraft; they self-organize into a mesh on landing. This is the production unit.
Planned
Estimated NRE $500k – $2M+
Unit cost at volume <$1 / node
Duration 12–24 months
Est. completion 2028+ (funding-dependent)

Can we achieve sub-$1/node unit economics at scale with full wafer-level integration?

Architecture
  • Custom RF CMOS die (~5 mm², 180 nm) + MCU die + PMIC die embedded in mold compound
  • On-package RDL routing and 2.4 GHz RDL antenna; no flex PCB
  • Thin-film PV bonded at wafer scale to top and bottom; SiO₂ hermetic passivation
  • Prototype partners: IMEC, CEA-Leti, IHP Microelectronics; production scale via ASE Group

"Phases I and II retired the technical risks. Phase III is a pure capital-scaling play. This is the inflection point where NetAnywhere becomes a deployable macro-environmental platform."
