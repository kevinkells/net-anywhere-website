Hardware De-Risk Roadmap

A phased strategy for validating the physics and manufacturing path of the NetAnywhere node — from breadboard through production-grade wafer-level packaging.

0
Phase 0 — Breadboard
1BLE — ESP32-C6 + Solar
In Progress

Does the solar harvest → store → relay loop work at all? What range and power numbers do we actually see?

  • 4-node BLE Coded PHY mesh relay demonstrated indoors
  • Outdoor range: ~35 m at +3 dBm, ~88 m at +20 dBm (1M PHY)
  • BLE + WiFi coexistence characterized; time-multiplex workaround confirmed
  • Solar wiring integration (Step 4) — harvest loop not yet closed on bench
  • Outdoor power-under-sun measurements pending

"We have a working 4-node mesh. We know our range-per-hop and power draw numbers from real hardware in the field."

0.5
Phase 0.5 — Compact PCB
Schematic Validation — Standard SMT
Next

Does the schematic close on a properly designed PCB, with a solar cell matched to near-target dimensions?

  • ~40–50 mm square, standard 2-layer FR4, QFN / SOIC packages
  • Solar cell sized to approximate the 25 × 25 mm target harvest area
  • Easily probeable — validates harvester topology, supercap sizing, and firmware before committing to HDI
  • Fast-turn fabrication: PCBWay or equivalent, ~$10/board, ~1 week

"The breadboard proves the physics; the 2-layer PCB proves the schematic. We de-risk the circuit design before committing to expensive HDI rules."

I
Phase I — HDI Micro-PCBA
The "Micro-PCBA Sandwich"
Planned

Does the physics close at the exact 25 × 25 mm X-Y form factor, holding in your hand?

  • 25 × 25 mm HDI PCB, WLCSP silicon (ESP32-C6 WLCSP or nRF52840 WLCSP)
  • PMIC: BQ25570 DSBGA or e-peas AEM10941; supercap: SMD EDLC 0.9 mm profile
  • Thin-film PV bonded to top and bottom faces; total thickness ~3.5–4.2 mm
  • Assembly: standard SMT pick-and-place at MacroFab or Screaming Circuits; ~$2–5k for 25–50 units

"We have a functional, scatter-deployable 25 × 25 mm node you can hold today. Shrinking from 4 mm to 1 mm is a solved engineering problem — we are not asking you to take a physics risk."

II
Phase II — Bare Die on Flex
Chip-on-Flex (CoF)
Planned

Can we hit the ~1 mm Z-axis profile required for aerodynamic scatter deployment?

  • 50 µm polyimide flex substrate with RDL antenna; bare dies via Micross / SemiDice
  • Energy storage: CAP-XX ultra-thin prismatic supercap pouch (~0.4 mm)
  • Passives: Silicon Capacitors (SiCaps) or 01005 for zero Z-height impact
  • Assembly at OSAT: QP Technologies or Promex Industries; NRE ~$30–50k
  • Total target thickness: ~0.95–1.15 mm

"Phase I proved the physics. Phase II proves the form factor. The supply chain here maps directly to the high-volume reel-to-reel manufacturing that hits $1/node."

III
Phase III — Custom Silicon
Fan-Out Wafer-Level Packaging (FOWLP)
Planned

Can we achieve sub-$1/node unit economics at scale with full wafer-level integration?

  • Custom RF CMOS die (~5 mm², 180 nm) + MCU die + PMIC die embedded in mold compound
  • On-package RDL routing and 2.4 GHz RDL antenna; no flex PCB
  • Thin-film PV bonded at wafer scale to top and bottom; SiO₂ hermetic passivation
  • Prototype partners: IMEC, CEA-Leti, IHP Microelectronics; scale via ASE Group
  • NRE: high (hundreds of thousands to millions); unit cost target <$1 at volume

"Phases I and II retired the technical risks. Phase III is a pure capital-scaling play. This is the inflection point where NetAnywhere becomes a deployable macro-environmental platform."
